\textbf{Theorem.}
Let $f:(A,\leq)\to(B,\leq')$ be an order embedding. Then the corestricted
map $f:A\to f(A)$ is bijective, its inverse exists, and both $f$ and
$f^{-1}$ preserve order.
\end{tcolorbox}

\begin{remark*}[Dependencies to state]
This capstone uses functions, images, inverse functions, induced suborders,
and order embeddings.
\end{remark*}

\begin{remark*}[Dependencies to prove]
Use injectivity of order embeddings, corestriction to the image, and the
definition of order-reflection.
\end{remark*}

\begin{remark*}[Dependency ceiling]
The capstone may use only results routed at or before this chapter.
\end{remark*}
