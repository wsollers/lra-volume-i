\begin{theorem*}[Chapter Synthesis]
Injectivity gives a bijection onto the image; order embedding gives an order
isomorphism onto the image; order-preservation pulls upper and lower sets back
to upper and lower sets.
\end{theorem*}

\begin{proof}[Professional Standard Proof]
\LRAProofBodyStart
Item (i) is \hyperref[thm:injective-bijection-onto-image]{Injective Maps Are
Bijections Onto Their Images}. Item (ii) is
\hyperref[thm:order-embedding-isomorphism-onto-image]{Order Embedding Is
Isomorphism Onto Image}. Item (iii) is the order-preserving part of
\hyperref[thm:preimage-upper-lower-sets]{Preimages of Upper and Lower Sets}.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Detailed Learning Proof]
\LRAProofBodyStart
The synthesis combines the three main repairs in the chapter. Changing the
codomain to the image fixes surjectivity. Adding injectivity gives an inverse
on the image. Adding order-reflection upgrades that inverse-on-image to an
order isomorphism. Monotonicity controls preimages of ordered regions.
\end{proof}
