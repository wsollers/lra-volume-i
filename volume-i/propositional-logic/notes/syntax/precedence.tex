\begin{remark*}[Convention: Parentheses and Precedence]
Fully parenthesized formulas are the official syntax. Precedence conventions
are only abbreviations for readability. The standard order, from tightest to
loosest binding, is:
\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.2}
\begin{tabular}{c l l}
\toprule
\textbf{Rank} & \textbf{Symbol} & \textbf{Convention} \\
\midrule
1 & parentheses & override all other conventions \\
2 & $\neg$ & negation binds tightest \\
3 & $\land$ & conjunction \\
4 & $\lor$ & disjunction \\
5 & $\to$ & conditional, right-associative \\
6 & $\leftrightarrow$ & biconditional \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
When in doubt, use parentheses.
\end{remark*}
